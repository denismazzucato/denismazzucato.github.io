10th December 2024, Ph.D. Thesis

Static Analysis by Abstract Interpretation for Quantitative Program Properties

Denis Mazzucato, under the supervision of Caterina Urban

The goal of this thesis is to develop formal techniques that make software more reliable and secure. The research employs a mathematical-based approach, called Abstract Interpretation, to understand and predict the software behavior. The thesis focuses on measuring how different inputs affect the software's performance and correctness. By identifying which input has the biggest impact, we can find potential problems or certify that the software works as expected. This research led to the development of three tools, Libra, Impatto, and Timesec, that help detect issues and verify the behavior of software. These tools have been tested in various scenarios, such as ensuring fairness in artificial intelligence systems and identifying security vulnerabilities.
